DESCRIPTION

This is a fine-scale, short term, process-based landscape evolution model using simulated erosion and deposition to generate a time-series of digital elevation models. This model uses a path sampling method to solve water and sediment flow continuity equations and model mass flows over complex topographies based on topographic, land cover, soil, and rainfall parameters. This either steady state or dynamic model can simulate landscape evolution for a range of hydrologic soil erosion regimes. It can be run as a detachment limited simulation or a dynamic simulation for a single rainfall event or a series of rainfall events. The time-series of digital elevation models generated by the simulation are registered in the temporal framework.

Dynamic model

This mode uses r.sim.water and r.sim.sediment to simulate erosion and deposition and evolve the digital elevation model. The change in elevation is a function of time, net erosion-deposition, and sediment mass density: 

change in elevation (m) = change in time (s) * net erosion-deposition (kg/m^2s) / sediment mass density (kg/m^3)

Detachment limited model

This mode for detachment limted soil erosion regimes uses r.sim.water and r.sim.sediment to simulate sediment flux and evolve the digital elevation model. The change in elevation is a function of time, sediment flux, and the mass of sediment per unit area: 

change in elevation (m) = change in time (s) * sediment flux (kg/ms) / mass of sediment per unit area (kg/m^2)
